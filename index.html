BIO

Queer Latino Astronomer, Stellar Spectroscopist, and Exoplaneteer. Now on the job market!
Born and raised in Kansas. Proud first-generation student.
I received my B.S. in Physics and B.S. in Mathematics from Kansas State University in May 2020. Currently, I am a graduate student working with Prof. Ian Crossfield in the KU ExoLab. My research focus is on dwarf star abundances, elemental and isotopic. I am particularly interested in how these dwarf star abundances can be used to test galactic chemical evolution models and how they may be used as chemical clocks to determine stellar ages. I also measure these abundances in exoplanet host stars to predict the formation history of their companion exoplanets.

Modeling Stellar Spectra & Deriving Elemental Abundances

Generating model spectra using MARCS stellar atmosphere models and TurboSpectrum spectral synthesis code to determine precise chemical abundances in a variety of cool dwarf stars

Testing Galactic Chemical Evolution Models

Dwarf star abundances provide key constraints for the chemical evolution of the local solar neighborhood.
